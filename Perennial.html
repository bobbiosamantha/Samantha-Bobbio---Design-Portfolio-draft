Perennial

All Your Sustainable Needs in One Place

What is Perennial?

Perennial is a mobile application that is available for both iOS and Android Platforms. Perennials goal is to push consumers to choose an eco-friendly life by providing the ease that ‘Amazon’ has. Users will be able to subscribe to any product so that they receive refills on all of their products weekly/monthly etc. Users will be able to shop all of Perennials private label products with ease as well as explore affordable options.

Brand Principles

SUSTAINABILITY

We are committed to saving our planet and want Perennial to be a space that makes it easy for users to convert to a Zero-Waste LifeStyle.

RELIABILITY

Switching to a Zero-waste Lifestyle can be difficult and expensive. we are here to provide a range of different home / beauty / kitchen products that can be refilled and sent to your door ASAP.

MINIMAL

Users will be able to navigate through our shop easily with a simple and intuitive design

Usability Testing

What Users Liked...

  • The color palette and image choices are very modern and suit the mission of the app/brand.
  • The type set is easy to read.
  • Clicking through for more info and purchasing is easy.
  • Reviews are shown, which helps me feel more comfortable with buying the products.

What Users Disliked...

  • On the sign up/sign in page the logos for quick sign-in are not centered.
  • Reviews could be higher up on the page or even in the browse all products section.
  • Could offer a side bar to browse different categories.

Style Guide

I wanted Perennial to have an earthy feeling that would represent the sustainable mission of the brand. I went with a light beige as the background color to set the main tone of the app. For accents I used white in order to bring attraction to the buttons and guide the user. I added a forest green accent for when I need to bring extra attention to important functions of the app. Reviewing typography, I was originally going to make the app use Big Caslon consistently but decided that it didnt have the clean look I wanted perennial to emulate so I switched to Avenir instead.

Final Screens

After some Typography and color challenges I landed on this design. I originally was going to make the apps background white but felt that was boring and decided to go for beige instead. I feel this makes the interface more interesting while prioritizing the earthy look. I also played with the main accent color being forest green instead of white but I found that made the design look clunky and messy. Lastly, settling on the correct way to seperate the checkput process was difficult. I found the best way was to seperate each step in order to not clutter the screen and keep the user on track.

Reflections...

  • The main thing I learned while creating this app is to never get attached to one version of your design because you should always be looking to edit and evolve your ideas. While creating this app I went through a lot of different color palettes and always felt strange when I wanted to switch it up because I was afraid of making the app too ‘different’ or just missing the way it used to look.
  • I also learned that with creating different stylistic choices its always good to have multiple eyes on it. Each style I made I showed to friends to get feedback and this helped me make decisions faster in addition to choosing a design that users would like more.
  • What helped me create the app and gain UI inspiration was doing deep research on the eco-friendly market. Doing an immense amount of research helped me create the app with ease as I was able to see UI trends as well as how to articulate sustainability goals.
